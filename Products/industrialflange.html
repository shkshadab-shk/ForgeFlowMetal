The Six Common Types of Industrial Flanges

  • Slip-on — to slide over a pipe while maintaining a somewhat tight fit
  • Weld Neck — connected to a pipe by welding the flange's neck to balance stress transfer for high-pressure applications
  • Blind — a bolted seal that closes off flow to a vessel or through a piping system
  • Lap Joint — a free rotating flange ideal for stub end fittings and applications that demand regular dismantling
  • Threaded — a non-welded flange specifically designed for pipes with external threads
  • Socket-weld — attached by inserting a pipe and applying a top weld for a smooth bore and inner flow

Industrial Flange Pressure and Temperature Standards

Pipe fitting suppliers not only adhere to global standards but also tailor their components to meet specific temperature and pressure requirements for diverse applications. Pressure classes and temperature ratings can be customized to ensure consistency within a particular customer's operational environment, such as in power generation stations, large process plants, and drilling systems.

Pressure classes are assigned numerical rankings, spanning from lightweight options like 75 or 125, to higher classes such as 350 or beyond. This wide range allows for precise selection based on the demands of the system and the application's needs.

Material Grades and Performance

Industrial flanges can be crafted in a wide range of material grades for custom performance. From Stainless Steel pipe fittings to exotic alloy flanges, every grade comes with its own set of benefits. Some common choices include:

  • Carbon Steel Flanges, the most popular industrial pipe fittings
  • Stainless Steel Flanges
  • Plate Flanges for waterworks applications and the petrochemical industry
  • Bar Flanges
  • Forged Spec Flanges
  • Titanium Flanges, for low weight and high strength
  • Aluminium Flanges, for lightweight applications
  • Exotic Alloy (Inconel, Hastelloy, etc.) Flanges, for high corrosion, high temperature, and specialty applications
