Reducing Flanges

What is a Reducing Flange

About Reducing Flanges

A reducing flange is characterized by a flange with a single specified diameter, featuring a bore of a different and smaller diameter. Apart from the bore and hub dimensions, the flange may have dimensions corresponding to the larger pipe size.

Reducing Flange can also be fabricated using a reducing spool with concentric or eccentric reducer & desired flange at every finish end OR it could be a single flange with an internal reduced bore to connect different sized fittings on either side. There are different types of reducing flanges including weld-neck, threaded, and slip-on.

How to Connect Reducing Flange?

Weld Neck and Slip-On: Connect the flange sides that match the higher diameter of the pipe and complete the welding method. Repeat the welding method for the smaller size of pipe on the other side of the reducing flanges. Now both sizes of pipes are connected through the reducing method. Take enough care to make sure that the inside diameters of the pipes are properly aligned with the ID of the flange on both sides.

Threaded Flange: Initially connect any 1 size of the pipe by threading to one side of the flange. Make sure that the pipe is inserted to reach the required depth inside the flange. Repeat the same method for the other side of the pipes.

In high-pressure and temperature applications, welding is done on the outer side of the pipe after threading to increase the strength of the joint. Designed for use in changing diameters in a piping system, a reducing flange consists of a flange with one specified diameter having a bore of a different and smaller diameter. Except for the bore and hub dimensions, the flange can have dimensions of the largest pipe size.

What is a Reducing Flange?

The reducing flange is engineered to accommodate changes in pipe size seamlessly. It features a primary flange with dimensions matching the larger pipe size (NPT), yet with a smaller bore matching the smaller pipe size (NPT). These flanges typically come in slip-on, threaded, blind, and weld neck variations.

Available across all pressure classes, reducing flanges offer an excellent solution for connecting pipes of different sizes. However, they should be avoided in situations where an abrupt transition might lead to undesirable turbulence, such as at a pump installation.

Uses and Advantages of Reducing Flanges

Reducing flanges offer the most efficient solution for connecting pipes of vastly different sizes. They provide a simple and economical method to join two pipes of different sizes, making them highly versatile for various applications. Typically available in various pressure classes, including up to #2500 class, reducing flanges ensure reliable connections even in demanding operating conditions.
